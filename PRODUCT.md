# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Compliance and procurement teams at importers of EUDR-regulated commodities (cocoa, coffee, palm oil, rubber), plus ESG/sustainability teams at sustainable investment funds who need verifiable environmental evidence for SFDR disclosures. Secondary: carbon market participants (future). They land on this page pre-purchase, often via outreach or a compliance-deadline search, evaluating whether PROVRB is credible enough to trust with regulatory-grade evidence.

## Product Purpose
A pre-Series-A marketing landing page for PROVRB, a Luxembourg-based SpaceTech SaaS that turns Sentinel-1/Sentinel-2 satellite data into continuous, audit-ready EUDR compliance evidence for agricultural supply chains. The page's job is to convert visitors into early-access sign-ups by establishing enterprise credibility fast, replacing the current "expensive consultants + occasional site visits" model with continuous, provable monitoring. Success = a visitor with no prior context believes this is well-funded deep-tech infrastructure, not a compliance consultancy or NGO, within the first screen.

## Brand Personality
Precise, confident, institutional. Voice is declarative and evidence-first ("Today companies say they're sustainable. PROVRB asks: can you prove it?"), never promotional or hedging. Should read as software a regulator, auditor, or fund compliance officer would trust on sight.

## Anti-references
Not a sustainability consultancy or NGO site (no soft stock photography, no earnest mission-statement tone as the primary register). No WordPress/template feel, no generic SaaS-marketing-site tropes (hero-metric blocks, identical icon-card grids, gradient text). No stock photos of "nature" or people; only abstract satellite/earth-observation and dashboard-derived imagery.

## Design Principles
- Evidence over adjectives: every claim implies "we can show you," mirroring the product's own value prop.
- Institutional restraint: enterprise software confidence, not startup-cute or NGO-warm.
- Deep tech, not decoration: satellite/orbit/radar imagery is functional visual language, not stock illustration.
- One accent, used with intent: gold/amber marks action and evidence states; forest green carries brand identity, not decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG 2.1 AA contrast targets. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` (already handled in index.css `[data-reveal]` and button transitions). Fully keyboard-navigable nav, anchor links, and contact form.
